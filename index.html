My Journey

  • Nashville Software School

    May 2016

    I began in Day Cohort 14 at Nashville Software School in May 2016. I wanted to get back into software development, and it was my best option at the time. It turned out to be my best option in general. Here I learned more that just CSS, HTML, and Javascript. I also learned many tools and techniques to be a good developer and work as a team. My favorite part of the program was organizing and helping my classmates during group projects.

  • Social Work

    May 2015

    After my VISTA contract ended I began working as a Peer Specialist at Park Center in Nashville, Tennessee. I primarily worked with Park Center members who lived in the agency's subsidized housing for people with mental illness. While at Park Center I gained a greater appreciation for working as a team to try and help people. I would like to continue to make contributions via my technical skills to the mental health field.

  • Americorps VISTA

    May 2014

    After studying at MTSU I decided to take a break from my STEM background and do a year of service with the Americorps VISTA program. I worked on a multi-agency collaboration that spanned both city government and non-profit agencies. My project was to help refine Nashville's system of getting services to those experiencing homelessness.

  • Middle Tennessee State University

    August 2012

    In August 2012 I started a Master's program at Middle Tennessee State University in compute science. While in the program I gained a strong theoretic background in software development and learned C/C++. One of my favorite classes was assembly language because it taught me to analyze every minute detail of even the most simple problems.

  • Rhodes College

    May 2012

    I graduated from Rhodes College in Memphis, Tennessee with a bachelors of science in chemistry in 2012. I also took as many gender studies and film studies classes as I could fit into my schedule due to my passion for these subjects.
